Six Brown Hands in a Basin

Full post can be found on Medium

My time in Grand Goave, Haiti was coming to an end. Instead of having my clothes washed for me I decided to wash them myself. After finding soap and a basin I grabbed my bag of dirty clothes and headed to the beach. Greg, Max, Jonas, Julius and Wal were some of the students we hung out with in Haiti. Wal filled a bucket of Well water for me and we walked a few steps from my hammock to the beach.

As Greg and Max walked by, they asked me in Creole if I was going to wash my clothes. I asked Julius to translate. He told them I was. must have been the funniest thing they heard all day because they erupted in laughter. I told them I could wash my clothes. I didn’t know how clean they would be, but I could do it. They watched as I got started. I was the afternoon entertainment and there was a growing audience.

“I hope I get my clothes clean so I look like I know what I’m doing!”
